About Me

Hi, I’m Kate! I’m an Applied Mathematics Research Associate at Newcastle University, working between the Cosmology and Quantum Fluids groups. My research falls under the umbrella of “Quantum Simulators for Fundamental Physics” and I’ve spent the last five or so years modelling early-universe phase transitions using finite-temperature, cold-atom analogue systems. Day-to-day, I use high performance computing resources to solve stochastic partial differential equations numerically. I’m currently in the process of transitioning from academia to industry, so if you’re looking for a programming expert with versatile communication skills and extensive experience of handling complex data, feel free to get in touch!
